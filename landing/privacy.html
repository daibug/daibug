Privacy Policy

We collect nothing.

daibug is a local developer tool. It has no server, no accounts, and no data collection of any kind.

Last updated: February 2026

Short version: All data stays on your machine. No telemetry, no analytics, no remote servers. The extension only activates on localhost and 127.0.0.1. Nothing leaves your computer.

What daibug collects

Nothing. daibug does not collect, store, transmit, or share any data about you or your usage.

The CLI tool

  • Runs entirely on your local machine
  • Binds the Hub HTTP server to 127.0.0.1 only — not accessible from the network
  • No outbound connections to any remote server
  • No telemetry, no crash reports, no usage metrics
  • No authentication, no user accounts, no registration required

The Chrome extension

  • Only activates on localhost and 127.0.0.1 URLs
  • Connects exclusively to the local daibug Hub running on your machine
  • Does not communicate with any external servers
  • Does not read, store, or transmit your browsing history, personal data, or credentials
  • No cookies set, no tracking pixels, no fingerprinting

Data flow

Browser events (console, network, DOM) captured by the extension are sent via WebSocket to the Hub running on 127.0.0.1. The Hub holds events in an in-memory buffer on your machine only. Your AI agent reads from this local buffer via MCP. No data ever leaves your computer.

Third-party services

daibug has no third-party integrations, no analytics scripts, and no CDN dependencies. The extension and CLI are entirely self-contained.

Open source

daibug is fully open source under the MIT license. You can audit the code at github.com/daibug/daibug.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, please open an issue on GitHub.
